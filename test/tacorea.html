      TaCOREA! Get it? A spelling error but it works. Tacorea is a small korean and mexican fusion taco shop similar to HRD and Kogi trucks (in LA). They do things a bit different though because they add more fresh vegetables to your burritos. A small shop with limited seat so you gotta be a poacher. Yup! Stand by a table and when they even make any motion of getting up, you swoop in and try to grab their table. California Burrito: The California part really shines in this burrito. We got it with bulgogi and the meat was also very flavorful and tasted very authentic. What made this burrito so good was the addition of tomatoes in it. It's simple but the freshness of the tomatoes transforms the burrito from a korean burrito to a more Cali feel to cut through all the rich and heaviness. Tator Tot Nachos: Tator tots piled high with guac, onions, crema, and cheese. I slowly enjoyed it more and more but at first bite, the cold guac over the tots was a bit strange due to the difference in temperatures. The kayne asada (no that's not a typo) was tender and flavorful. what I liked the most was that near the center, the tots got a bit soggy and the started to melt in your mouth. Spicy Pork Rice Bowl: A huge bowl filled with spicy pork, a cooked egg, veges, and smothered in sauce. After having the tots and burrrito it was tough to keep going but the flavor of the pork was very good. The veges like the tomatoes in the burrito cut through the heaviness. It was more korean than the other dishes and although flavorful, I say not as good as the burrito. Tacorea is a great place for a meal. I say it's a different concept from HRD so they can't be really compared and they are equally as good. The wait I hear can get pretty long but it is worth it.

        TaCOREA! Get it? A spelling error but it works. Tacorea is a small korean and mexican fusion taco shop similar to HRD and Kogi trucks (in LA). Stand by a table and when they even make any motion of getting up, you swoop in and try to grab their table.

        California Burrito: The California part really shines in this burrito. We got it with bulgogi and the meat was also very flavorful and tasted very authentic. What made this burrito so good was the addition of tomatoes in it. It's simple but the freshness of the tomatoes transforms the burrito from a korean burrito to a more Cali feel to cut through all the rich and heaviness.

        Tator Tot Nachos: Tator tots piled high with guac, onions, crema, and cheese. I slowly enjoyed it more and more but at first bite, the cold guac over the tots was a bit strange due to the difference in temperatures. The kayne asada (no that's not a typo) was tender and flavorful. what I liked the most was that near the center, the tots got a bit soggy and the started to melt in your mouth.

        Spicy Pork Rice Bowl: A huge bowl filled with spicy pork, a cooked egg, veges, and smothered in sauce. After having the tots and burrrito it was tough to keep going but the flavor of the pork was very good. The veges like the tomatoes in the burrito cut through the heaviness. It was more korean than the other dishes and although flavorful, I say not as good as the burrito.

        Tacorea is a great place for a meal. I say it's a different concept from HRD so they can't be really compared and they are equally as good. The wait I hear can get pretty long but it is worth it.
